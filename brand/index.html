Brand Style Guide

Voice & Tone

Our voice is that of a seasoned executive: confident, concise, and focused on the bottom line.

Direct & Action-Oriented

We don't use fluff. We focus on verbs: build, execute, scale, convert.

Yes: "We cut through the chaos and focus on the only metric that matters—growth."
No: "We help brands navigate the complex marketing landscape to find synergy."
Business-First

We speak the language of the C-Suite (P&L, unit economics, ROI), not just marketing jargon.

Key phrase: "We keep marketing in lockstep with your P&L."
Forward-Looking

We embrace the future (AI, automation) but ground it in timeless business principles. Technology is a tool, not the pitch.

Usage Rules

Do

  • Use Libre Baskerville for headlines and the stacked wordmark
  • Use Instrument Sans for body copy, UI, labels, and navigation
  • Maintain charcoal-on-paper or paper-on-charcoal contrast
  • Apply grain overlay at exactly 5% opacity
  • Use generous whitespace — padding and margins should let content breathe
  • Treat the logo as a logotype in Libre Baskerville — no graphical mark
  • Always pair the stacked wordmark with the tagline: GROWTH MARKETING PARTNERS
  • Use design tokens from @theme — never hardcode hex

Don't

  • Mix serif and sans arbitrarily — italic Libre Baskerville closers are the exception, not a free pass
  • Use colors outside the defined palette
  • Alter letter-spacing on the stacked wordmark or tagline
  • Stretch, rotate, or skew any logo lockup
  • Separate the wordmark from its tagline in the stacked lockup
  • Use "Growth demands clarity" as brand copy — it is not an approved tagline
  • Use "SSP" in public-facing copy — it is for internal use only
